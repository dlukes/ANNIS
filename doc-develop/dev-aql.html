Complete List of Operators

The ANNIS Query Language (AQL) currently includes the following listed operators.

direct precedence: "."

(Illustration)
A & B & #1 . #2

For non-terminal nodes, precedence is determined by the right most and left most terminal children.

indirect precedence: ".*"

(Illustration)
A & B & #1 .* #2
A & B & #1 .4 #2
A & B & #1 .2,4 #2

For specific sizes of precedence spans, .n,m can be used, e.g. .3,4 - between 3 and 4 token distance

direct dominance: ">"

(Illustration)
A & B & #1 > #2
A & B & #1 >secedge #2
A & B & #1 >secedge[func="OA"] #2

A specific edge type may be specifed, e.g.: >secedge to find secondary edges. Edges labels are specified in brackets, e.g. >[func="OA"] for an edge with the function 'object, accusative.

indirect precedence: ".*"

(Illustration)
A & B & #1 >* #2
A & B & #1 >2,4 #2
A & B & #1 >secedge[func="OA"] * #2
A & B & #1 >[func="OA"] 2,4 #2

For specific distance o dominance, >n,m can be used, e.g. >3,4 - dominates with 3 to 4 edges distance.

identical coveragee: "_=_"

(Illustration)
A & B & #1 _=_ #2

Applies when two annotation cover the exact same span of tokens.

inclusion: "_i_"

(Illustration)
A & B & #1 _i_ #2

Applies when one annotation covers a span identical to or larger than another.

overlap: "_o_"

(Illustration)
A & B & #1 _o_ #2
A & B & #1 _ol_ #2
A & B & #1 _or_ #2

For overlap only on the left or right side, use _ol_ and _or_ respectively.

left aligned: "_l_"

(Illustration)
A & B & #1 _l_ #2

Both elements span an area beginning with the same token.

right aligned: "_r_"

(Illustration)
A & B & #1 _r_ #2

Both elements span an area ending with the same token.

directly near: "^"

A & B & #1 ^ #2

Elements are next to each other in any order (A-B or B-A). For non-terminal nodes, adjacency is determined by the right most and left most terminal children.

indirectly near: "^*"

A & B & #1 ^* #2
A & B & #1 ^4 #2
A & B & #1 ^2,4 #2

For specific sizes of proximity spans, .n,m can be used, e.g. .3,4 - between 3 and 4 token distance in either order (A...B or B...A)

labeled direct pointing relation: "->LABEL"

(Illustration)
A & B & #1 ->LABEL #2
A & B & #1 ->LABEL[annotation="VALUE"] #2

A labeled, directed relationship between two elements. Annotations can be specified with ->LABEL[annotation="VALUE"]

labeled indirect pointing relation: "->LABEL *"

(Illustration)
A & B & #1 ->LABEL * #2
A & B & #1 ->LABEL 4,7 #2

An indirect labeled relationship between two elements. The length of the chain may be specified with ->LABEL n,m for relation chains of length n to m.

left-most child: ">@l"

(Illustration)
A & B & #1 >@l #2

right-most child: ">@r"

(Illustration)
A & B & #1 >@r #2

common parent node: ">$"

(Illustration)
A & B & #1 >$ #2

common ancestor node: ">$*"

(Illustration)
A & B & #1 >$* #2

arity: "#x:arity=n"

(Illustration)
A & #1:arity=2

Specifies the amount of directly dominated children that the searched node has.

length: "#x:length=n"

(Illustration)
A & #1:length=2

Specifies the length of the span of tokens covered by the node.

root: "#x:root"

(Illustration)
A & #1:root

node x is the root of a subgraph (i.e. it is not dominated by any node)
